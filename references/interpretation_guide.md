# Interpretation Guide

Reference for turning script output into an actual reading. This is meant to
keep interpretation grounded and specific, not to hand you generic
horoscope-column copy.

## Philosophy: psychological astrology, not fortune-telling

Treat the chart as a symbolic map of temperament, tension, and tendency —
closer to a Jungian/archetypal framework than literal prediction. The most
useful astrological writing describes *patterns the person can recognize in
themselves*, not events that will happen to them. When in doubt, phrase
things as "this placement often shows up as..." rather than "you will...".

This framing is also more honest: a Mars-Saturn square doesn't cause
conflict with authority figures, but people with that aspect often
*recognize* a pattern of friction with authority once it's named — that's
the actual value being offered.

## The Big Three (always lead with these)

- **Sun sign** — core identity, ego, what the person is consciously growing
  toward. The house it's in shows *where* that identity expresses itself.
- **Moon sign** — emotional instinct, what feels safe, unconscious reaction
  patterns. Often more accurate-feeling to people than the Sun sign because
  it's how they actually *feel*, not how they present.
- **Ascendant (Rising sign)** — the interface with the world; first
  impressions, instinctive approach to new situations. Requires accurate
  birth time — flag this if time was approximate.

## Houses (the *where*)

1. Self, identity, how you initiate
2. Resources, self-worth, what you value
3. Communication, immediate environment, siblings
4. Home, family roots, emotional foundation
5. Creativity, romance, self-expression, children
6. Daily work, health routines, service
7. Partnerships, marriage, open conflict with others
8. Shared resources, intimacy, transformation, death/rebirth themes
9. Belief systems, higher education, travel, philosophy
10. Career, public reputation, authority
11. Community, friendships, long-term goals
12. The unconscious, isolation, self-undoing patterns, spirituality

## Aspects (the *tension or ease* between two points)

- **Conjunction (0°)** — fusion; the two planets act as one force, for better or worse
- **Sextile (60°)** — easy opportunity, needs some activation to use
- **Square (90°)** — friction that forces growth; usually the most psychologically
  significant aspect in a chart, the place where the person has done real work
- **Trine (120°)** — natural ease, can also mean a talent that's never been
  tested or developed because it came too easily
- **Opposition (180°)** — polarity to be balanced, often projected onto
  other people before it's recognized as internal

Tight orbs (under ~2°) are more strongly felt than wide ones (3°+). Always
mention the orb when discussing a specific aspect — a 0.5° square is a core
life theme, a 2.8° square is a faint echo of one.

## Writing the reading

1. Open with Sun/Moon/Ascendant — 2-3 sentences each, tied together into a
   coherent picture of the person, not three disconnected paragraphs.
2. Cover the 2-4 tightest-orb aspects — these are doing the most psychological
   work in the chart.
3. Mention any notable concentration (e.g. 4+ planets in one sign/house —
   this person's energy is heavily channeled into that life area).
4. For transits: name what natal planet/point is being "activated," describe
   the type of energy the transiting planet brings, and frame it as a season
   or theme rather than a single predicted event.
5. For synastry: lead with the relationship score and its category, then
   pick the 3-4 most significant cross-aspects (Sun-Moon, Venus-Mars,
   anything to the Ascendant) to explain the texture of the connection —
   strengths and friction points both.
6. Close with something useful and forward-looking, not just descriptive.
